# Chrome Web Store Screenshot Shot List

Required: ≥1 screenshot at **1280×800** (preferred) or 640×400. Recommended: **3–5 shots**, varied states. The first shot is the most important — it's the hero in search results.

Save final files here as `01-*.png`, `02-*.png`, etc.

## Setup before shooting

1. Load unpacked extension from `extension/dist/` (chrome://extensions → Developer mode → Load unpacked)
2. Sign in to transcribed.dev cloud mode (or use local mode if you want OSS shots)
3. Run a few transcriptions ahead of time so the Library has real-looking entries
4. Use a clean Chrome profile if possible — no other extensions visible
5. Open Chrome at exactly **1280×800** window size, or resize the side panel + content area to compose nicely; you can crop after
6. Pick a recognizable but not-niche video (e.g. a popular TED talk, podcast, or product launch — avoid copyrighted music videos for review hygiene)

## Shot 1 — HERO: side panel open, "Transcribe" ready

**State:** YouTube video page on the left, side panel on the right, video detected, "Transcribe" button visible, library list collapsed or showing one or two prior transcripts.

**Why:** This is the single most important shot. It tells the whole story in one frame: "extension lives next to YouTube, one click to transcribe."

**Avoid:** YouTube logo prominently visible in screenshots — Google review can flag it. Crop or blur the YouTube wordmark if it's huge.

## Shot 2 — Transcription in progress

**State:** Side panel showing progress bar / spinner, status text like "Transcribing…" or the queue indicator with a job running.

**Why:** Demonstrates the action — the user sees the product *doing* something.

## Shot 3 — Completed transcript with Summarize button

**State:** Side panel showing a finished transcript scrolled to the top, with the "Summarize with Claude / ChatGPT" affordance visible.

**Why:** This is the differentiator vs. every other transcript Chrome extension. The summarize keyword is what users search for. **Highest-value secondary shot.**

## Shot 4 (optional) — Library / recent transcripts

**State:** Library view of the side panel, showing 4–6 prior transcripts with titles and timestamps.

**Why:** Demonstrates persistence and "I own my transcripts" — different value prop from competitors who just summarize and discard.

## Shot 5 (optional) — Settings / connectors

**State:** Settings panel showing Cloud/Self-hosted mode toggle and the Notion / Obsidian connector switches.

**Why:** Shows extensibility and the destination integrations for power users who care.

## Composition rules

- 1280×800 PNG, no JPEG artifacts
- Real content, not Lorem Ipsum — transcripts and titles should look believable
- Side panel should be at its natural Chrome width (around 380–420 px); don't stretch
- Dark Chrome theme is fine and matches the extension's dark UI
- Don't show personal email addresses, account screens, or anything that doxxes your test account
